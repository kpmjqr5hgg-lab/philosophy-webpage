An Introduction to Philosophy

Philosophy helps us understand ourselves and the world we live in. Throughout history many incredible thinkers have contributed to how we see truth, knowledge and morality.The word itself means "love of wisdom" from the Greek language. The earliest trace of philosophy is around the 6th century originating in ancient Greece.

Jump to some well-known philosophers

Core Branches

  • Metaphysics - nature of reality, existence, and the universe.
  • Epistemology - theory of knowledge and belief.
  • Ethics - Moral philosophy, right and wrong.
  • Logic - principles of correct reasoning.
  • Aesthetics - philosophy of art, beauty and taste.
  • Political Philosophy - government, society, justice, rights.

Well-known Philosophers

  • Socrates - "The only true wisdom is in knowing you know nothing."
  • Aristotle - "Knowing yourself is the beginning of all wisdom"
  • Plato - "The unexamined life is not worth living."
  • John Locke - "All mandkind...being all equal and independant, no one ought to harm another in his life, health, liberties or possessions."
